INTERSTELLAR

STELLAR NAVIGATION CHART · A TRAJECTORY FROM EARTH TO TON 618

ABOUT

A three-dimensional plot of the local stellar neighbourhood, built from the European Space Agency's Gaia DR3 catalogue — the most precise survey of stellar positions ever made. The closest 50 parsecs around our Sun are rendered here, each plotted in true position using parallax-derived distance.

A dashed red bearing traces the trajectory from Earth to the selected destination — by default TON 618, a hyperluminous quasar 4.2 billion light-years away. Select any star and the bearing re-aims to it.

Click any star, planet, or beacon to read its profile. Switch view modes at the top: Nav, Spectral, Minimal, and Sol System.

METHOD

Positions queried via the Gaia Archive (ADQL), converted from RA/Dec/parallax to Cartesian XYZ (ICRS). Colours from Gaia's BP-RP index; sizes from G-band magnitude. Planetary bodies are stylised and not to scale.
