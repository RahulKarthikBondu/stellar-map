STELLAR NAVIGATION CHART · A TRAJECTORY FROM EARTH TO TON 618

ABOUT

A three-dimensional plot of the local stellar neighbourhood, built from the European Space Agency's Gaia DR3 catalogue — the most precise survey of stellar positions ever made. Of its 1.8 billion stars, the closest 50 parsecs (~163 light-years) around our Sun are rendered here, each plotted in true position using parallax-derived distance.

A dashed red bearing traces the trajectory from Earth to TON 618 — a hyperluminous quasar 4.2 billion light-years away.

Click any star, planet, or beacon to read its profile in the Selected Object panel. Switch view modes at the top: Navigation for the clean chart, Spectral for full star colours, Minimal for the trajectory alone, Sol System to step into our home neighbourhood.

METHOD

Stellar positions were queried via the Gaia Archive (ADQL) and converted from sky coordinates (RA, Dec, parallax) to Cartesian XYZ in the ICRS reference frame. Star colours come from Gaia's BP-RP index, mapped through an approximate blackbody spectrum. Sizes follow G-band apparent magnitude on a logarithmic scale.
